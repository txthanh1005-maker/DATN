\section*{ABSTRACT}
    \thispagestyle{empty}

The increasing severity of extreme weather events exposes the inherent vulnerability of traditional distribution grids, rendering centralized and linear energy management models inadequate. To enhance physical resilience and economic efficiency under severe disruptions, this thesis proposes a highly rigorous, bottom-up spatiotemporal energy management framework for Multi-Microgrid (MMG) systems. The proposed architecture fundamentally synergizes three core mechanisms: SOCP AC-OPF constraints for voltage security, ATC for decentralized, privacy-preserving multi-agent coordination, and MPC to robustly navigate system uncertainties through a rolling horizon. 

By stress-testing the architecture against severe islanding scenarios, the simulation reveals several significant findings. Most crucially, the framework guarantees a 100\% survival rate for Critical Loads during grid failures. Economically, the P2P network capitalizes on stranded renewable energy, successfully manifesting a ``Negative Premium'' where post-fault operational costs are counterintuitively lower than normal pre-fault grid-connected operations despite severe generation shortages. Furthermore, the rigorous mathematical formulation exposes two key physical phenomena. First, the Tie-line Congestion Paradox demonstrates that the ATC consensus pricing ($\lambda$) remains decoupled from extreme VOLL escalations, being constrained strictly by the KKT thermal limits without causing economic collapse. Second, the Voltage Paradox highlights the necessity of the AC-OPF integration, wherein the algorithm is forced to execute extreme active power load shedding as a last resort to suppress transit currents, thereby neutralizing significant $I^2X$ reactive power losses and averting catastrophic voltage collapse below the 0.90~p.u. hard boundary. Ultimately, this thesis establishes a computationally tractable, fault-adaptive, and physically secure P2P energy trading framework, forging a resilient paradigm for the next generation of decentralized power systems.

\subsection{Conclusion}
This thesis successfully developed a fault-adaptive, P2P energy trading framework to enhance multi-microgrid resilience, anchored by four core academic pillars:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Algorithmic Architecture:} The integration of a ``Bottom-up Spatiotemporal 3-Mode State Machine'' was accomplished. ATC algorithm demonstrated remarkable computational tractability, achieving rapid convergence (within a maximum of 91 seconds, which is strictly well within the 15-minute operational dispatch interval of the MPC rolling horizon, ensuring real-time tractability) during severe structural grid shocks, substantially leveraging Warm-start techniques.
    \item \textbf{Physical Resilience and AC-OPF Necessity:} The effectiveness of the AC-OPF integration was mathematically validated. The framework identified that transit currents induce significant reactive power losses ($I^2X$), directly leading to severe voltage collapse. Consequently, the system proactively executes normal active power load shedding to curtail the current $I$, successfully preventing the voltage profile from violating the 0.90~p.u. lower bound.
    \item \textbf{Market Pricing and the Tie-line Congestion Paradox:} The study uncovered the ``Tie-line Congestion Paradox''. The ATC consensus price ($\lambda$) remains completely decoupled from the local price explosion (Value of Lost Load, VOLL) at energy-deficit nodes, due to the KKT bounds of the transmission cables.
    \item \textbf{System Security and Graceful Degradation:} The framework guarantees the protection of 100\% of critical loads. It achieves a dual economic-resilience benefit termed the ``Negative Premium'' by dynamically routing P2P exchanges to liberate ``Stranded Energy''. This is synergistically combined with a proactive ``Graceful Degradation'' mechanism, intentionally curtailing normal loads to secure the system's operational integrity.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Future Work}
To fundamentally address the physical and algorithmic limitations observed in this study, future research should focus on three primary directions:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Overcoming Conservative BESS State-of-Charge (SOC) Retention:} To mitigate the conservative MPC BESS discharging behavior (driven by the strict end-of-horizon SOC penalty constraints), introducing an RL-based SOC Penalty will encourage more proactive BESS discharging. Additionally, employing Stochastic MPC will provide robustness against the inherent uncertainty of renewable energy generation.
    \item \textbf{Hardware and Reactive Power Support:} To resolve the issue where intermediary microgrids suboptimally shed active power solely to sustain voltage levels, future iterations will integrate dynamic reactive compensation devices (e.g., STATCOM, SVC) or Smart Inverters equipped with Volt-VAR control. This will independently supply the necessary reactive power ($Q$) support.
    \item \textbf{Dynamic Topology Reconfiguration:} Integrating a Dynamic Switch Control mechanism will enable real-time network reconfiguration. This will allow the system to autonomously isolate physical fault zones and establish backup routing paths, thereby optimizing power flow and further enhancing the structural resilience of the multi-microgrid network.
\end{itemize}
